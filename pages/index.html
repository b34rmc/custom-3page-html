What Is Devpipeline

Devpipeline?

Let's start by what devpipeline isn't. Devpipeline isn't a bootcamp. Bootcamps are a 12 week course that cost around 12-15 thousand dollars. In those 12 weeks they Plan to make you a production ready developer. Unfortunatly, 12 weeks just isn't enough to be a production ready developer. 12 weeks is just enough time to get a grasp on the basics. You've made a few programs at this point and are feeling more competent in your developing abilities. Although your feeling more confident you still don't feel anywhere near capable of getting a job. There's a reason for that, It's because you're not. That's why people out of bootcamps have a very low success rate for getting jobs. Most of the time they are doing freelance work trying to build up a resume.

Devpipeline is also not a college. Colleges are 4 years for software development. The people who come out of college have the skills they need to get a job and are competent developers and ready for the field. Theres just one problem, they don't have a high success rate getting jobs either. Why's that? Because they don't have any on the job experience. So what does DevPipeline do different?
